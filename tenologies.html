#1. Blockchain

  1. About Blockchain

    The history of blockchain started in 2008 when Satoshi Nakamoto, whose identification is still anonymous to the wide public until this day, published the white paper of Bitcoin . Basically, blockchain can be seen as an online ledger that records information in chunks or blocks or a list of records. It is described as “a purely peer-to-peer version of electronic cash ... based on cryptographic proof instead of trust” . Blockchain uses five supplementary technologies to form its unique properties

      • Secure: Asymmetric cryptography and cryptographic hash functions

        • Asymmetric cryptography

          The two different keys are used for encrypting and decrypting data. While the public key is used for encrypting the information, the other key called private key is to decrypt the information as demonstrated in the figure below.

        • Cryptographic hash functions

          The Cryptographic hash functions takes information input in an arbitrary size and generate the output in a fixed-length size so called hash. This hash is function is nearly one way only which means generate the hash from data is easy but by knowing the hash, there is no way to work backward and figure out what the information is. Additionally, any changes to the data will generate the different hash, indicating its uniqueness like the fingerprint. Each block will contain data, a hash, and the hash of previous block as being shown in the Figure below

      • Ledger: Append-only databases

        As an append-only databases, blockchain can be seen as a ledger recording transactional information such as sender, receiver, value, time, etc.

      • Distributed: Peer-to-peer networking

        Peer-to-peer (P2P) network is an interaction without the need of involving the centralized server (party). Compared to a client-server, P2P is decentralized, less vulnerable to single point of attack.

      • Incentive compatible: Game theory

        As a result of proof-of-work, it creates a game called mining where miners from all around the world compete to solve a mathematical puzzle until they find a hash with leading zero bits to satisfy the condition. Therefore, this process requires a lot of computational energy (hashing power), causing controversial debates about bitcoin mining.

      • Consistent: Consensus algorithms

        The transaction is validated by all users in the network indicating its consensus. Therefore, a single person will not be able to change the information without the agreement of the whole network. When an individual joins the network, one will receive the full copy of the chain.

    Based on the five above mention properties, blockchain can solve many frauds and challenges in business field, especially finance and supply chain.

  2. Impact

    1. Application of Blockchain on Finance

      Thanks to its consensus algorithms that each user on the network keeps a copy of it, blockchain can solve the double spending problem on finance . Secondly, in recent financial system, any transaction requires trust because there must be the third party to facilitate while in blockchain there is no central authority needed (decentralized network) making it higher integrity . Therefore, blockchain in the next five or ten years could change the landscape of financial system dramatically. There is several evidence showing that few governmental organizations around the world are taking a further step toward cryptocurrency which is a result a blockchain technology. For example, in Philippine, the Union Bank uses blockchain technology, specifically Ethereum smart contract, to build their payment financial system connecting with rural areas because more than 65% of the population are unbanked . The second governmental action is observed in Australia since the Australian Commonwealth Bank becomes the first bank to officially offer crypto services to customers this November (2021) . Last but not least, the most important event gaining great attention among cryptocurrency investors and stakeholders, The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) offers an encourage sign to cryptocurrency once approving the exchange of Bitcoin Futures ETF in this October (2021) . In short, these strong evidence indicate for the bright future of blockchain development in Finance Industry.

    2. Application of Blockchain on Supply chain management

      Due to its immutability and decentralization, blockchain technology is able to solve the problem of traceability and information feasibility in supply chain management industry. As a supply chain sophisticatedly consists of resources, human, activities, information exposing to the three different movement flows from suppliers to customers, it requires an extraordinary administration during the whole shipment . Hence, a centralized supply chain is more likely to have corruption, fraud, and tampering which are addressed by using blockchain to increase visibility and transparency. More importantly, the issue of origin information is very essential in agricultural and food supply chain, thus the application of blockchain could protect consumers from counterfeit product . In more detail, by providing precise and confirmed origin of food, it evidently increases the enterprises’ reputation, leading to higher profit and return on investment .

#3. Robots

  1. About Robots

    • Robots are an influential element in today's manufacturing industry . This is because the robot works simultaneously with other equipment, peripheral equipment, and manufacturing machines , where robot is integrated with the system. In more detail, robots can skillfully perform many arduous tasks and accurate operations without the general safety and comfort necessities required by humans .
    • People now will easily determine which career path is best for them based on their abilities, hobbies, and the sort of work that they enjoy within the next three years . The usage of online and offline software packages, for example, has made tax preparation more accessible and convenient than ever before. Non-resident aliens can save time by using tax preparation software and online services. However, data entry labor performed by employees in offices and even from home is expected to become obsolete soon. It is conceivable that robots will be trained to take over conventional data entry jobs and responsibilities.
    • Brief history

      The scope of what is called robotics is expanding in tandem with technological advancement. In 2005, automotive factories were the home of 90 percent of all robots who were entrusted with the construction of autos at the time. These robots are mostly composed of mechanical arms that are charged with welding or screwing certain elements of an automobile together. In today's world, we're witnessing a more developed and extended definition of robotics that encompasses the research, construction, and deployment of bots that explore the Earth's harshest environments, law-enforcement robots, and even robots that aid in practically every aspect of healthcare .

    • How Robots work

      There are two sorts of robots: independent robots and dependent robots :

      • Autonomous robots are those that are capable of operating independently and without the need for human operator control. These normally require more intensive programming, but they enable robots to take the place of people in a variety of risky, mundane, or otherwise impossible activities, ranging from bomb dissemination and deep-sea navigation to industrial automation and everything in between Independent robots be the greatest disruptive force in society, destroying low-wage occupations while opening up new opportunities for economic growth and development.
      • The term "dependent robot" refers to a non-autonomous robot that interacts with people to augment and complement the activities of humans. Advanced prostheses that are controlled by the human mind are one sort of dependent robot that has been created. This is a relatively new form of technology that is continually being pushed into new uses.

      Power is provided via an electric power supply, which is utilized by the vast majority of robots. The two most popular types of electric power supply for robots are grounded electrical power plugs for stationary robots and internal battery units for mobile robots, with the former being more frequent.

      Both the "brain" system of robots and the sensory system are intricately intertwined and work in tandem to perform their respective functions. Similarly, depending on the job of the robot in question, robot "brain" systems may be either very simple or very complex. If a sensor system detects anything, a basic "brain" system would send a signal to the robot's muscle system, directing it to come to a complete halt and stop.

    • Pratical application
      • Agricultural: agricultural robots are supported by technologies such as global navigation satellite systems, satellite earth observation, and satellite communications, which are poised to be at the forefront of the third agricultural revolution. This will not be accomplished by displacing farmers, but by increasing the farmer's added value while maximizing yield and optimizing the use of natural resources .
      • Transportation and logistics industry: Robotics is enabling the expansion of the transportation and logistics business. Robots can drive independently to a destination while being surrounded by radio transmitters, vision cameras, magnetometers, LiDAR, lasers, digital maps, navigation systems, and sensors to detect obstacles. They can also calculate their exact location and route while being surrounded by radio transmitters .
      • Social care: robots and autonomous systems, in conjunction with artificial intelligence, linked data, and digital infrastructure, have the potential to completely transform how social and medical care is given to the elderly and disabled people .

  2. Impact

    When it comes to things like scheduling and organizing correspondence, an assistant's skills are much more valuable than when it comes to answering simple questions from visitors. The time saved by having robots take over such routine tasks allows workers to devote their attention to concerns that have a tangible influence on the company's bottom line, matters that need the use of greater brain capacity and more ingenuity to resolve . The reality is that robots are far more efficient than humans in many situations, particularly when it comes to things like producing products. Not only are robots capable of doing tasks with greater precision, hence reducing the amount of time and resources lost, but they are also capable of performing tasks quicker (and for a longer time) than people. While this may have a negative influence on the employment that people depend on, it also has the benefit of lowering the price of products since it lowers the cost of production. A larger number of individuals can then afford such items, whether they be automobiles, clothes, or computers as a result of the increased availability of those goods. For example, a coffee shop that employs robots to prepare coffee may (in principle) cut the price of a latte since less labor is required to operate the business in general. As an extension of the previous point, this is a good place to start. Because robots will be able to take over at least part of the tasks that humans do daily, people will have more free time on their hands in the future. As an alternative to thinking about robots just in terms of the human, they could eventually replace, it is useful to consider the advantages they will offer.

#4. AI

  1. About AI

    • The rapid development of AI and machine learning has helped develop voice recognition technology, which increasingly penetrates every area of life . From the above, along with the analysis below, Hybrid Technologies will vividly demonstrate to you why voice control technology should be considered as the current technology trend not only by the men and women. IT majors or those with a passion for AI, but businesses are also aggressively upgrading and optimizing systems using voice technology, also known as "Virtual Assistants". More specifically, a virtual assistant (also known as a digital assistant, voice assistant or AI assistant) is a task-oriented programming application that recognizes human voices and executes spoken commands by the user . Its foundation is AI and its productivity relies on storing millions of words and millions of phrases. Unlike the first speech recognition devices that scientists were working on in the 40s and 50s of the last century, modern digital assistants are not restricted by a language pattern or words. certain vocabulary.

      • Virtual assistant at home: The category belongs to home automation (also called smart home), a system that allows to control appliances, lighting, electricity, appliances and other things that fill our homes. Smart homes require an internet connection and are part of the Internet of Things (IoT).
      • Virtual assistants at work: virtual assistants for the workplace, can be used in addition to communication with building automation, as a standalone application that is incorporated into the routines of everyone on the team.
    • Brief history

      Virtual assistant is a new concept in the field of technology and software, first known to the world in 1961 at the Seattle World's Fair, in the form of a device called IBM Shoebox, with the ability to recognize voice. By 1970, the US Department of Defense and its agency DARPA had developed a "Harpy" tool that could recognize about 1000 words, equivalent to the vocabulary of a 3-year-old child. It was not until the 1990s that virtual assistants were officially researched and applied on personal computers by leading manufacturers such as Microsoft, IBM, Philips and Lernout & Hauspie. In particular, the launch of the first smartphone IBM Simon in 1994 laid the foundation for the intelligent virtual assistants we know today. In 2011, the first modern digital assistant installed on a smartphone was Siri, which was introduced as a feature on the iPhone 4S. From 2017 to now, virtual assistants have begun to attract the attention of users thanks to practical applications, especially with strong investment from large corporations such as Amazon with Alexa, Google with Google Assistant or Apple with Siri.

    • How Virtual Assistant works

      Voice assistant applications work on the Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) system. ASR systems record speech and then break it down into phonemes, which are later processed into text . A phoneme (not a word of a syllable) is a basic unit of measurement for human speech recognition. Phonemic recognition yields better results than word decoding, since the end-user tends to analyze the word as a standalone unit ignoring contextual constraints. No matter what kind of speech recognition software you might use, it is based on ASR. In regard to how to create a virtual assistant software, the most important thing to do is familiarize yourself with how ASR works. In a nutshell, the process begins with the device collecting the sound with the microphone. Recorded voice waveforms are passed straight to audio analysis, which is performed at three different levels :

      • The sound model, which represents what phonemes are pronounced and what words these phonemes complete;
      • Pronunciation model, analyzing the pronunciation of phonemes, having any stress or other peculiarities of the vocal apparatus to capture the phonetic variation of speech;
      • Language modeling, which aims to find contextual probabilities depending on which phonemes are recorded.
      All data is processed by AI without human interaction, reducing error rate by using machine learning algorithms . The voice waveform data is then transmitted to the decoder, where it is finally converted to text for further use such as commands or dictation. Artificial intelligence gives modern voice assistant apps the freedom not to rely on a limited vocabulary, but to use cloud storage with millions of alternative words and phrases. In other words, machine learning makes applications listen to the entire speech, not each individual word. That way, voice apps analyze context and probabilities to determine what you're trying to say.

  2. Impact

    The beginning of a new digital era has been strongly affecting human society . Changing people’s lives in a completely different way, making every task much simpler and easier. The world will receive many benefits from this technology. Human can save our time and thinking from tons of trifles. But there are still some potential things threat the develop of human from Virtual Assistant.

    • Threat of leaking information: Researchers from the international security company Check Point have just said they have found a systemic vulnerability appearing in the virtual assistant Alexa, paving the way. Allowing hackers to access and steal voice history data and set up Alexa skills without the user’s knowledge. This means that conversations with Alexa involving users’ personal data can be hijacked and used by hackers to break into their Amazon devices. A typical approach a hacker might use is to create a legitimate link to a website used to track Amazon shopping information. After the user clicks the link, there is a “shortcut” set up that allows the hacker to swap out the usual skills the user has installed on their Alexa device with malicious ones.
    • Virtual assistants can be easily attacked by ultrasonic waves: Security researchers have just proven that Alexa, Siri, Cortana and Google Assistant can easily be asked to do things without the owner’s knowledge by ultrasonic commands. Like any waveform, sound covers a wide range of frequencies, with some of which actually audible to humans. Any sound wave below 20 Hz and above 20 kHz cannot be heard by our ears, meaning it does not exist. Even so, phone mics and smart speakers are perfectly capable of detecting sounds beyond this range. That’s why they can be attacked by ultrasonic commands, leaving the user completely at risk.
