Project ideas

#1. Overview

This project aims to build a synthesized solution for COVID 19 problem – an Automated COVID-free checking system for indoor environment which requires the sophisticated involvement of many types of technologies. The system will include application, AI cameras, sensors whose detailed designed algorithm will be explained in the later parts of this paper. The primary use of this system will be installed for indoor environment that helps us check whether the people are COVID-free without using currently available quick test kit costing more than two hundred VND each time. The system function will have real-time COVID check thus also saving our time. As being able to detect SARS-CoV-2 and its variants, the system helps to reduce the chance of asymptomatic spreading, safeguarding frontline workers and the community and avoiding outbreaks.

#2. Motivation

The SARS-CoV-2 virions (COVID-19 pandemic) – one of the most concerned human’s health crises starting from January 2020 – has hit the world harshly in almost every single field thus leading to a potential great global recession since people all around the world are directed to shut down a large number of community activities. Vietnam was of the top nations performing good COVID-19 prevention policies back in early 2021 that was acknowledged by both many renowned global news and peer-reviewed journals as a success story . Despite being a role model in COVID response strategies, Vietnam still struggles to cope with its fourth wave of the pandemic that many cities tightened the restriction policies and even closed from April 2021 until October 2021 . Eventually, the nation with “0 COVID” policy turned to “living with COVID in a new normal” policy which again proves COVID is inevitable that human must deal with. However, until the drug treatment for COVID is approved, the advent of something could help human to recognized COVID is demanded. Evidently, the high mortality rate resulting in consecutive disruptions entails severe consequences across the globe . It is fact, Hospitality and tourism industry, for instant, is the most suffering sector since the strict strategies from Vietnamese government of border closures, travel restrictions, quarantine was applied early the pandemic started . Only in this sector, the economic loss hit US$5.9–7.7 billion in only three months , which is attributed to 78.7% drop in international tourism .

Yet, the current available approaches to prevent the COVIS spread is not strong enough to resume social activities safely due to several reasons. The quick test kit and PCR test, for example, are costly and un-reusable, and not real-time . Firstly, once a guest book the hotel staycation, the person is able to provide the evidence of ‘positive test’ (no infection) by the quick test kit and PCR test within 24 hours. Yet there is no guarantee that this result reflects his/ her true status when the person arrives at the hotel. There is still chance that the person is infected during the travel to hotel, indicating its inability of giving real-time result. Secondly, these tests can only be applicable less than 72 hours, meaning un-reusable after this duration. Thirdly, these tests are costly.

As a consequently, the urgent need of a real-time and cost-advantage detecting COVID-19 system is obvious. Therefore, this project acts as a critical contribution to such an uncertainty and volatile situation of COVID that could help us to resume community activities, flattening the curve global recession.

#3. Description of technology

  1. Devices

    The whole system is made up of three primary technologies: instant biosensor, AI camera, application (administrative system)

    • Instant Biosensors (2 components: the personal tag & reader)

      An advanced nanomaterial biosensor. This sensor can detect antibodies specific to SARS-CoV-2. The method of identifying specific antibodies to viral antigens is one of the two main methods to detect infectious diseases today. Antibody testing can help doctors and researchers verify whether a vaccine is working during treatment and identify asymptomatic cases in a certain population.

      In addition to testing capabilities, this basic sensor also helps to accurately quantify the body’s immune response to the new vaccine, allowing doctors to make accurate diagnoses. Antibody detection is performed through an electrochemical reaction in a hand-held microfluidic device, after which results are quickly displayed through a simple smartphone interface. Scientists have used the latest advances in materials and manufacturing, such as 3D printing of nanoparticles, to build a device that can rapidly detect Covid-19 antibodies . Aerosol injection 3D printing additive manufacturing technology was used to ensure the efficiency and accuracy of the test equipment. Small, inexpensive gold micrometallic electrodes are printed at the nanoscale using aerosol droplets, bonded together by thermal sintering. This method creates a rough, irregular surface, increases the surface area of the micropoles and electrochemical reaction, the area where the antibodies can bind to the antigens coated on the electrode from there, helping to detect quickly and accurately the risk of COVID-19 infection in the environment.

      This idea of using sensor to detect COVID in real-time has long been researched and attempted by many professors coming from universities around the world. One of the earliest and most successful projects is from RMIT University . After this successful research, the trial program was run in Melbourne. More detail, the sensor tag’s size is as small as an air pod case which is convenient for people to bring it everywhere all the time. The personal tag will be synthesized with the Ministry of Health (MOH) to also get the status of the individual’s vaccination. Moreover, this personal sensor tag is also equipped with Bluetooth connection to minimize physical touch as much as possible and easier to share your health status to a friend’s phone without swiping or touching. The readers will be installed in the room doors. Before entering, people must exhale to their personal sensor tag then swiping their personal tag to the checkpoint readers. The data from reader will be communicate to the cloud system that people can monitor via application.

      The currently available technological advancement allows us to have thermal camera fever detection but not yet the COVID one. Therefore, developing this to a further level by incorporating cough sound checking, we are able to have an AI camera that could detect SARS-CoV-2 and its variants as people walk by the camera. To achieve this product, a high level of training AI is required so that it is capable of recognizing COVID in forced cough. Since many people are asymptomatic which is 25% of the infection sample with 95% confidence interval according to a systematic review and meta-analysis by Alene et al. this is essential to make the system more practical and reliable in real-life application. Regarding the practical ability of sound cough detecting, there are many successful researched projects and implementation including the ones by RMIT University and by MIT (Massachusetts Institute of Technology). Additionally starting from mid 2021, Vietnam started on this race of COVID sound checking on forced cough according to Saigoneer .

    • AI camera:

      A high level of training AI is required so that it is capable of recognizing COVID in forced cough. The camera attached is used to measure the density of vehicles and people in each time frame, thereby providing statistics on vehicle traffic in each time period so that administrators can have an appropriate approach treating COVID-19.

  2. Central control

    The system adopts Zigbee/LoRa for two-way communication between sensors and control system. That means one master device will be attached at one light, then the slave devices will connect through the master one.

  3. System Management Application for administrators

    The user accounts will be managed by the government. There is a decentralization in users due to their position and power.

    All the data will be collected and cleaned to visualize on the dashboard of the Application. The users can view the reports, status and comprehensively manage all the system.

    The governmental institutions can use data from this app to effectively monitor the situation thus having the right and appropriate responses to COVID spread.

    Mobile version is also provided for convenient use. User can check others’ health status by scanning their QR or sensor tag Bluetooth.

#4. Tools and technologies

  1. Softwares
    • The System Management Application will be designed by CSS, HTML, JavaScript, NodeJS, Java for web version.
    • The database will be created and managed by MySQL, and hosting through Digital Ocean.
    • Python, OpenCV,… for AI functions, training Machine and receive signals from hardwares.
    • Using GitHub for corperation.
    • Server to train AI
  2. Hardwares
    • Zigbee: E18-MS1PA2-IPX Module Zigbee CC2530 for transmitting signals.
    • Sensors: Covid-19 sensor for each person; It may be displayed in a room or worn as a personal identification badge.
    • Micro Nano Research Facility is needed for the R&D of detecting viruses on breath
  3. Building Application

      React Native , which is an open-source UI framework, will be employed to build the application. One of the reasons React Native is preferable is its convenience as it can be applied for both IOS and Android using only one code base rather than using Swift for IOS and using Kotlin or Java for Android, leading to time-saving.

#6. Outcome

Once the initial trial of this project successes, a higher propose to implement piloting program in a small specific place will be the next step before it could be nationwide adopted. More specifically, RMIT University Vietnam Saigon South Campus could be an ideal environment to test the system as there are technological readiness to support the implementation. The demonstration is shown in Figure 21 that sensor readers will be installed in every entrance point, AI camera will be installed in every critical and high traffic point. Furthermore, inside a building, the senser readers will be installed in every room (Figure 22) so that we can make sure people’s status before granting access for them to the space. By doing this, we are creating a safe environment for every person in such space.

Figure 21: Illustration of sample installation of the system in an environment
Figure 22: Illustration of sample installation of the system inside one building

At the first stage, after successfully piloting at RMIT SGS campus, the collaboration with other organizations to further expand the pilot program could be attempted to test its effectiveness and practicability before introducing the system to the public. Therefore, it could be targeted to reach at least 3000 users from three organizations at the end of piloting. The more users, the higher trustworthiness our system is proved. If this project succeed, further action could be proposed to improve the users experiences as well as incorporating more detecting features.

In the future, even when the COVID drug treatment is approved, this system is still strongly believed to be beneficial as it helps us to recognize the COVID person more effectively and efficiently. Hence, the action to be taken will be more meaningful to cope with this illness. Furthermore, besides the practical purpose recognizing COVID, the success of such system is supposed to pave the way for further research to the invention of more systems addressing infectious diseases, and any respiratory related diseases.
