Interest in IT

    I has been building a great enthusiasm for business analytics since I started pursuing Logistics and supply chain industry as it is increasing essential in any business these days. Besides, many logistics and supply chain businesses faced technology disruption thus being less competitive and more challenging to thrive. It also requires me to work with various data sets and deal with tons of data sources in this industry which prompts me to attempt further studies on data-related fields. Eventually, I found IT is such a fascinating world empowering the business field a lot. Furthermore, I intend to pursue post-graduate study in Australia obtaining Master of Business Analytics. Thus, these foundation courses play a key role for me to have a strong base beforehand. More specifically, owning such technical skills of programming language and Machine Learning enable me to conduct optimization modelling, casual analytics, predictive analytics, etc. that current available applications (Microsoft Excel, BI tools) still have restrictions. Additionally, programming languages allows me to write my own mathematical modelling when I conduct further study where I try to solve complex problem in Logistics field. In my previous literature review research, I wrote a paper scrutinizing 20 different modellings in peer-review articles to solve the same Logistics-related issue. It was obvious to me that without knowledge of programming language, I am rarely capable of understanding such sophisticated modelling, not to mention writing them. Consequently, my dream of learning IT-related issue has been strengthening.   As RMIT is globally recognized and has a decent study infrastructure which supports students to achieve the most optimal outcomes, I believed that with an academic background graduated from RMIT I am capable of working a diverse position out there in the job market. In other words, with not only academic performance but also extra-curricular activities at RMIT, I am equipped with ready for work and life skill set increasing my employability.   I am about to finish my undergraduate degree (Bachelor of Logistics and Supply chain Management) at RMIT Vietnam, and I intend to obtain further study. Hence, I decided to enroll all my GE courses (General Elective) for courses in IT major. This is because these courses will be essential and related to my further study as a Master of Business Analytics. Therefore, I look forward to diving into more knowledge of the IT world. Moreover, by taking this course, it helps me to build better network with those who are from IT background which ultimately could contribute to my success in the future. Besides, it might open more chance for me to seek their competencies and expert advice when I start up my business in the near future.
